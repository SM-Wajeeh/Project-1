WE CARE ABOUT YOUR NAILS

NAIL CARE TIPS & TRICKS

Essential Nail Care for Healthy, Beautiful Nails

-KEEP YOUR NAILS DRY AND CLEAN-

  • Wash Hands Regularly:
    Clean your hands frequently to remove dirt, bacteria, and germs that could cause nail infections.


  • Dry Nails Thoroughly:
    After washing your hands, ensure your nails are completely dry to prevent moisture buildup, which can lead to fungal infections.


  • Prevent Infection:
    Keeping nails clean and dry reduces the risk of developing bacterial or fungal infections.


  • Avoid Prolonged Exposure to Water:
    Prolonged contact with water can soften nails and cause them to break, so be mindful of wet conditions.


  • Use Antibacterial Soap:
    Use an antibacterial soap when washing your hands to further reduce the risk of infection.

-TRIM NAILS REGULARLY-

  • Use a Nail Clipper:
    Trim nails with a sharp, clean clipper to maintain a neat length.


  • Trim Straight Across:
    Cut nails straight across to avoid ingrown nails.


  • Avoid Trimming Too Short:
    Don’t cut nails too short to prevent pain and infections.


  • Trim Dry Nails:
    Trim nails when they are dry to avoid bending or tearing.


  • Maintain Even Length:
    Keep nails evenly trimmed for a balanced appearance.

-MOISTURIZE YOUR NAILS AND CUTICLES-


  • Apply Cuticle Oil or Hand Cream:
    Use nourishing oils or creams to hydrate cuticles and nails.

  • Prevent Dryness and Cracking:
    Moisturizing prevents dry, brittle nails and cracked cuticles.

  • Promote Nail Health:
    Regular moisturizing supports strong, healthy nails.

  • Use Daily:
    Moisturize regularly, especially after washing hands or using hand sanitizer.

  • Massage into Cuticles:
    Massage oil or cream into cuticles to encourage healthy growth

-AVOID BITING YOUR NAILS-

  • Prevent Nail Bed Damage:
    Biting nails can harm the nail bed and hinder nail growth.

  • Reduce Risk of Infection:
    Nail biting introduces bacteria to the nails and surrounding skin, increasing the risk of infections.

  • Avoid Misshapen Nails:
    Regular biting can lead to misshaped, uneven nails over time.

  • Preserve Nail Health:
    Stopping the habit helps maintain the integrity and appearance of your nails.

  • Consider Alternatives:
    Replace nail biting with healthier habits like using a stress ball or chewing gum

-PROTECT YOUR NAILS-


  • Wear Nail Protective Gear:

    Protect your nails from damage by wearing protective gear like nail clippers, gloves, and nail polish removers.

  • Keep Your Nails Clean:
    Regularly clean your nails to remove dirt, grime, and buildup, which can lead to breakage or infections.

  • Use Nail Protective Pads:
    Apply nail protective pads to your hands and face to protect your nails from damage.

  • Avoid Over-The-Counter Nail Polish:
    Over-the-counter nail polish can be harmful to your nails and may cause breakage or staining.

  • Consider Alternatives:
    Consider using natural nail products, like nail polish removers or gentle nail polish, to help protect your nails.

-MAINTAIN BALANCED DIET-

  • Consume Vitamin-Rich Foods:
    Include vitamins like A, C, E, and biotin in your diet for healthier nails.


  • Biotin for Strong Nails:
    Biotin (found in eggs, nuts, and legumes) is particularly beneficial for promoting strong, thick nails.


  • Eat Protein-Rich Foods:
    Protein helps in the production of keratin, the primary building block of nails, so incorporate lean meats, beans, and fish.


  • Stay Hydrated:
    Drinking enough water helps keep your nails hydrated and less prone to breakage.


  • Include Omega-3 Fatty Acids:
    Foods like salmon and flaxseeds provide omega-3s that help maintain nail flexibility and strength.


  • Focus on Whole Grains:
    Whole grains provide essential minerals like zinc, which can improve nail health and prevent brittleness.
